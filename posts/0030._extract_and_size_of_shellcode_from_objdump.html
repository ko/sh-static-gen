--- categories: coding date: 2011/10/11 00:03:45 guid: http://kenko.co/?p=131 tags: shellcode, objdump title: Extract and size of shellcode from objdump permalink: extract-and-size-of-shellcode-from-objdump --- Because I am not going to do this by hand for a second time. $$code(lang=bash) objdump -d /tmp/ko | grep -v '<.*>:' | grep -v 'file' | grep -v '\..*:' | cut -f2 -d: | cut -f1-11 -d' ' | tr -s '\t' ' ' | sed -s 's/ $//g' | sed -s 's/ /\\x/g' | sed -e ':a;N;$!ba;s,\n,,g' | sed -e 's,^,",' | sed -e 's,$,",' $$/code Thank you, me. Oh, don't forget to replace that /tmp/ko with whatever you want. Also, append the following for the length of your shellcode: $$code(lang=bash) ... | awk '{c += gsub(s,s)}END{print c}' s='x' $$/code
